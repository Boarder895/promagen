# Call 3 Baseline Run — Result-Reading Guide

**Purpose:** How to read the artefacts the baseline run produces, what "good" and "bad" look like, and how to tell a platform problem from a pipeline problem.

**Companion docs:**
- `01-preflight-checklist.md` — how to run the baseline
- `03-decision-matrix.md` — what to build next based on what you read here

---

## 1. The artefacts, in order of importance

1. **`reports/harmony-pass-2-triage.md`** — primary. This is the whole exercise.
2. **Per-platform scores JSON** — needed to sanity-check the triage and to feed the mechanical scorer.
3. **Mechanical scorer output (R01–R10)** — tells you *why* a platform degraded, not just *that* it did.
4. **Run log** — only touch this when something is wrong.

Read them in that order. If the triage looks clean, you may not need to open the JSON at all.

---

## 2. Reading the triage table

The triage markdown (generated by `generateTriageMarkdown` in `src/lib/call-3-harness/triage.ts`) has the following structure:

```
# Harmony Pass 2.0 — Triage Report

**Computed:** <timestamp>
**Platforms:** 41
**Global mean baseline:** <N>
**Global mean optimised:** <N>

| Bucket | Count |
| 🟢 Green (≥50%) | <N> |
| 🟡 Amber (20–49%) | <N> |
| 🔴 Red (<20%) | <N> |

## Per-Platform Triage
| Platform | GPT? | Baseline | Optimised | Gain | Ceiling (src) | Headroom | Fraction | Bucket |
```

### Column-by-column

| Column | Meaning | What "good" is | Red flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Platform** | Platform ID from `profiles.json` | — | Unknown ID → profiles/runner mismatch |
| **GPT?** | `GPT` = GPT-backed Call 3 / `Det` = deterministic | Matches your intent for that platform | Unexpected `Det` on a platform you expected GPT on (or vice versa) = wiring drift |
| **Baseline** | Call 2 assembled score (pre-Call-3) | Should match prior harmony-pass numbers within noise | Wildly different baselines vs last harmony pass → Call 2 or scorer drift, not Call 3 |
| **Optimised** | Call 3 output score | ≥ Baseline | < Baseline = regression |
| **Gain** | `Optimised − Baseline` | Positive, and roughly proportional to platform headroom | Negative on > 3 platforms = systemic issue |
| **Ceiling (src)** | Theoretical max for platform. `M`=measured, `E`=estimated, `F`=fallback | `M` for Green calls, `E` acceptable for Amber | Everything `F` → ceiling estimation is falling back and triage can't trust itself |
| **Headroom** | `Ceiling − Baseline` (room Call 3 could have filled) | > 5 pts = meaningful room to work in | < 2 pts = platform near ceiling already, small gains are fine |
| **Fraction** | `Gain / Headroom` as a percentage | ≥ 50% = Green | < 20% = Red |
| **Bucket** | 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴 verdict | — | See §3 |

### The headroom fraction, in plain English

> **Jargon:** *headroom fraction* = of the points Call 3 theoretically **could** have added, what percentage did it actually add?

Example: Baseline 88, ceiling 95, optimised 92. Gain = 4. Headroom = 7. Fraction = 4/7 = **57%** → 🟢 Green.

Same baseline/ceiling but optimised 89. Gain = 1. Fraction = 1/7 = **14%** → 🔴 Red.

**Why this metric and not raw gain:** a platform with a baseline of 94 and ceiling 95 has only 1 point of headroom. A "+0.5 gain" there is actually 50% of the possible work — that's a real win, not a rounding error. Raw gain would mis-rank it as worse than a platform that gained +3 from a 70 baseline.

## 3. Bucket thresholds and what they mean

Hardcoded in `triage.ts` as:

- 🟢 **Green (≥ 50%)** — Call 3 is paying its cost. Keep GPT, refine the builder.
- 🟡 **Amber (20–49%)** — marginal. Test a deterministic-only path and compare.
- 🔴 **Red (< 20% or negative)** — Call 3 is degrading or wasting. Switch to deterministic or pass-through.

**These thresholds are unvalidated.** They are architectural guesses from the build plan. One of the outputs of this baseline run is whether the 50/20 split actually clusters usefully, or whether (say) everything piles into Amber and the bands are useless.

**When to suspect the thresholds themselves:**
- > 80% of platforms are in a single bucket → thresholds are not discriminating. Capture for ChatGPT review. Do **not** retune them blindly.
- Fractions distribute as a blob around 30–40% with no natural gaps → the buckets are artificial. Note it.
- Everything Green → either Call 3 is genuinely excellent, or the ceiling estimator is too generous. Spot-check 3 Green platforms against the per-scene data.

## 4. Per-scene regression warnings

Below the main table, the triage emits a section like:

```
## Per-Scene Regression Warnings
These platforms have a positive mean gain but negative gain on at least one scene:
- **<platform>** — <sceneId>: assembled <N> → optimised <N> (<gain>)
```

**This is the most important section in the whole report.** A platform can look 🟢 Green on mean gain and still be quietly destroying one scene type. That's the Trafalgar Square pattern from the handoff brief.

### How to read it

For each flagged line, note:
1. **Which scene category** it belongs to (look up the `sceneId` in `data/scoring/test-scenes.json`: `stress_test`, `outdoor_dramatic`, `indoor_character`, `abstract_stylised`)
2. **Which platform family** — is the same scene regressing on multiple platforms? (→ scene-weighted problem)
3. **How big the drop is** — 1–2 points = noise candidate; 3+ points = real regression

### Regression vs noise — the rule

- **Noise:** single-scene drop of 1 or 2 points on a single platform, with mean gain still positive. Flag it. Do not act.
- **Real regression:** ≥ 3 point drop, OR same scene regresses on ≥ 3 platforms, OR mean gain is negative.

**If in doubt, screenshot it and send to ChatGPT for a second opinion** — that's the review pattern Martin uses, and this is exactly the kind of judgement call it's designed for.

## 5. Reading the mechanical scorer output (R01–R10)

The mechanical scorer runs 10 rules. From `src/lib/call-3-harness/mechanical-scorer.ts`:

| Rule | What it checks | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **R01 OUTPUT_NOT_EMPTY** | Call 3 didn't return empty string | Catastrophic wiring failure. Must pass 100%. |
| **R02 CEILING_COMPLIANCE** | Output respects platform `maxChars` | Exceeds = gate is broken |
| **R03 NO_CATASTROPHIC_SHORTENING** | Output not shrunk absurdly vs input | Catches the "GPT over-shortens" pattern that's still unsolved |
| **R04 SUBJECT_SURVIVAL** | Main subject anchor survived | Critical anchor loss = unusable output |
| **R05 COLOUR_SURVIVAL** | Colour anchors survived | Lower severity than subject but still a quality marker |
| **R06 NO_INVENTED_CONTENT** | Call 3 didn't hallucinate details not in input | Paired with the negative intelligence engine |
| **R07 SYNTAX_CONSISTENCY** | Output matches platform syntax family (CLIP vs NL) | Wrong syntax = builder/platform mismatch |
| **R08 NEGATIVE_NO_CONTRADICTION** | Negative prompt doesn't contradict positive | Negative engine sanity check |
| **R09 RETRY_EFFECTIVENESS** | When retry fired, it actually recovered anchors | Directly measures whether Phase 8 earns its cost |
| **R10 TRANSFORM_ACTIVITY** | At least one deterministic transform made a visible change | Catches dead transforms (brief parked item) |

### What to do with rule output

The **absolute** numbers matter less than the **pattern**.

- **R01 or R02 fail anywhere** → stop. Something is broken. This is a pipeline bug, not a quality issue.
- **R03 fires on > 10 platforms** → the unsolved GPT shortening problem is active. This is the `maxChars > 2.5× idealMax` issue already known.
- **R04 fires at all** → subject anchors are dying. Check the regressing scenes; they likely share a subject pattern.
- **R06 never fires** → either Call 3 is exceptionally clean, or the rule is too narrow to catch real hallucination. Flag for "rule doesn't work", not "Call 3 is perfect".
- **R09 fires < 30% when retry was triggered** → retry protocol is spending money for no gain. This is a direct input to the decision matrix.
- **R10 fires on CLIP platforms specifically** → transforms are dead on CLIP. Relevant to the "skip Call 3 for CLIP groups" pending decision.

## 6. Platform problem vs pipeline problem

Critical distinction. The fix is totally different for each.

### Platform problem (one platform misbehaves)

**Signals:**
- One platform is 🔴 Red while peers in the same group are 🟢 Green
- Same platform fails R04 repeatedly, others don't
- Per-scene regressions all cluster on one platform
- Log has no errors, run completed cleanly

**Means:** the platform's DNA profile, builder config, or syntax family is wrong. Fix the platform, not the pipeline.

### Pipeline problem (systemic)

**Signals:**
- Multiple unrelated platforms show the same regression
- R01 or R02 failures anywhere
- The same scene regresses across many platforms (scene problem, arguably a third category)
- Run log contains rate-limit, timeout, or network errors
- Baseline scores don't match the prior harmony pass within expected noise

**Means:** the bug is in Call 3 itself, the scorer, the runner, or the test harness. Do **not** start editing platform profiles to "fix" it — you'll embed the bug.

### The scene problem (third category)

**Signal:** the same `sceneId` regresses on ≥ 3 unrelated platforms.

**Means:** the transform chain has a scene-specific bug (the Trafalgar "quality-position" cap issue is this pattern). Platform-level fixes won't help — the fix is in the transforms.

## 7. Red flags that mean "stop, something is broken"

Stop reading the triage and investigate if you see any of these:

1. **R01 OUTPUT_NOT_EMPTY fails anywhere** — Call 3 is returning empty. Wiring bug.
2. **Every platform in one bucket** — triage is not discriminating. Fractions are broken or ceilings are uniform.
3. **All ceilings sourced as `F` (fallback)** — estimator is falling back every time; the numbers are fiction.
4. **Global mean baseline differs by > 5 points from the last harmony pass** — Call 2 or scorer drifted. Baseline itself is unreliable. Triage on a shifted baseline is meaningless.
5. **Runner exited 0 but triage file missing** — runner exited before writing. Check log tail.
6. **Same platform appears twice** — dedup is broken in the runner. Rerun after fixing.
7. **Baseline = Optimised on > 20 platforms** — Call 3 is a no-op. The gate is rejecting everything, or the transform chain isn't firing.
8. **41 platforms but only N < 41 rows** — platforms were silently skipped. The log will say why.

## 8. What to screenshot for ChatGPT review

ChatGPT is your independent scorer. Give it what it needs to do the job:

1. **Full triage markdown table** (the bucket summary + the per-platform table, not the whole report)
2. **Per-scene regression warnings section** verbatim
3. **Mechanical scorer rule counts** (rule → pass/fail counts per platform where it fired)
4. **3 concrete before/after examples** — pick one Green, one Amber, one Red. Full assembled prompt + optimised prompt + score delta for each.
5. **The global mean baseline and optimised numbers** (so ChatGPT can sense-check magnitude)

Ask ChatGPT three specific questions:
- "Are the Green/Amber/Red thresholds clustering sensibly on this data?"
- "Do the per-scene regressions look like noise, a platform bug, or a transform bug?"
- "Which mechanical rule pattern most deserves investigation first?"

**Do not ask ChatGPT to pick which extra to build.** That's what the decision matrix is for, and it's yours to own.

## 9. What success actually looks like

A **healthy** baseline run has:

- Baseline scores within ±2 points of the previous harmony pass for every platform
- A spread of bucket assignments across 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴 (not all clumped)
- R01 + R02 at 100% pass
- R10 firing on > 80% of platforms (transforms are doing something)
- Per-scene regression warnings present but clusterable (i.e. they tell a story, not random noise)
- Global mean gain positive and non-trivial (> 2 points)
- Total run cost matches your dry-run extrapolation

If all of that holds, you have a trustworthy baseline. Go to `03-decision-matrix.md`.

If any of it doesn't hold, **the baseline is the problem, not the thing the baseline is measuring.** Fix the baseline first, re-run, then read again.
